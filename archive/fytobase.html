Welcome to Fytobase

This website aims to be a resource for understanding the phytochemicals (non-nutrient constituents) in regular foods. These molecules can:

  • Contribute to the culinary properties we enjoy in our meals or take into account during our cooking
  • Have pharmacological properties which may prove useful for DMPK scientists
  • May have health benefits - many of which are intimately linked to their agricultural methods of production (e.g. organic) or cooking
  • Explain how our body interacts with xenobiotics from a more evolutionary perspective

This website is a work in progress. The following images describe the design problems I am working through.

Note that the relationships would be best suited to a bidirectional lookup/analysis. For example, a user may wish to query all vegetables with phytochemical X, or they may wish to query all phytochemicals in vegetable Y. The database must be easy to:

  • Update manually with complex (and often long/spelling-sensitive) text without a major time cost or the need to learn complex commands
  • Extract conclusions from data easily once the database has been sufficiently established
  • Future-proofed, easy to back up and transferrable in the event of a better suited database environment
  • Accessible via an API to other programs/languages for manipulation e.g. injection of data into webpage
  • Difficult to corrupt by "butter-fingered" human users!
